# OKR Scoring Rubric

Reference for scoring Key Results at end of quarter.

## KR Scoring Scale (0.0 - 1.0)

| Score | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 0.0 | No progress | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 0% or negative |
| 0.1-0.3 | Minimal progress | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 2-6% |
| 0.4-0.6 | Partial delivery | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 8-12% |
| 0.7 | Strong delivery (aspirational sweet spot) | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 14% |
| 0.8-0.9 | Near-complete delivery | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 16-18% |
| 1.0 | Full delivery (committed expectation) | Target: 20% growth. Actual: 20%+ |

## Grading Thresholds by Type

### Aspirational OKRs

| Grade | Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Green | 0.7+ | Stretched hard, delivered meaningfully |
| Yellow | 0.4-0.6 | Made progress but fell short — diagnose why |
| Red | <0.4 | Significant miss — was the target wrong or the approach? |

**Calibration check:** If aspirational OKRs consistently score 1.0, targets are too easy. Increase ambition.

### Committed OKRs

| Grade | Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Green | 1.0 | Delivered as required |
| Yellow | 0.7-0.9 | Near miss — why didn't we close the gap? |
| Red | <0.7 | Significant miss — triggers root cause analysis |

**Root cause analysis for committed misses:**
1. Was the target realistic given constraints?
2. Did dependencies deliver?
3. Was the approach correct but execution fell short?
4. Did context change (market, priorities, resources)?
5. Should this have been aspirational, not committed?

## Objective Scoring

Objective score = average of its KR scores.

```
O1: Build a world-class onboarding experience
  KR1: Reduce time-to-first-value from 14 days to 3 days → Score: 0.7
  KR2: Increase 30-day activation rate from 40% to 65% → Score: 0.5
  KR3: Reduce onboarding support tickets by 50% → Score: 0.8

  Objective Score: (0.7 + 0.5 + 0.8) / 3 = 0.67
  Grade: Yellow (aspirational)
```

## Confidence Tracking Scale (Weekly)

| Confidence | Meaning | Action |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| 9-10 | Will definitely hit | Monitor only |
| 7-8 | On track, minor risks | Continue current approach |
| 5-6 | At risk, needs attention | Discuss in team check-in |
| 3-4 | Likely to miss without intervention | Escalate — change approach or adjust target |
| 1-2 | Will miss | Decide: accept miss, change target, or emergency pivot |

**Flags:**
- Confidence drops 3+ points in one week → immediate discussion
- Confidence stays below 4 for 2+ consecutive weeks → trigger target adjustment conversation
- Confidence stays at 10 for 4+ weeks → target may be too easy

## Common Scoring Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Correct Approach |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Binary scoring (0 or 1) | Loses nuance. Partial progress matters. | Use the full 0.0-1.0 scale |
| Inflating scores | "We did a lot of work" ≠ hitting the target | Score against the number, not the effort |
| Ignoring context | Sometimes a 0.3 in a crisis quarter is heroic | Note context in scoring, but don't change the number |
| Averaging across objectives | Hides individual objective performance | Report both objective scores and overall average |
| Scoring tasks as complete | "We launched it" scores 1.0 even though outcome wasn't measured | Score the outcome, not the output |
